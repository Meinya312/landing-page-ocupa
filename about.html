Office of the City Urban Poor Affairs

The Office of the City Urban Poor Affairs (OCUPA) is a government agency under the City Government of Lucena responsible for addressing the needs and concerns of the urban poor sector. Its primary function is to assist underprivileged families by providing access to socialized housing, community development programs, and related support services. OCUPA manages beneficiary information, evaluates housing applications, coordinates with various sectors, and ensures that qualified individuals receive appropriate assistance. The office plays a crucial role in organizing urban poor communities, maintaining updated records of beneficiaries, and monitoring the status of on-site and off-site housing projects across the 33 barangays of Lucena City. OCUPA also handles documentation, verification of eligibility, and the preparation of monthly and annual reports that guide decision-making within the local government. Through its administrative and field operations, the office serves as the central channel for ensuring that services reach the families and communities that need them most.

History

The Office of the City Urban Poor Affairs, formerly known as the Urban Poor Affairs Division (UPAD), was established in 1989 during the term of former Mayor Cesar Zaballero. It was created in response to the growing need for an organized system that would support socialized housing initiatives for underprivileged and homeless citizens of Lucena City. The office was formed to help uplift living conditions by providing secure and affordable housing options along with access to basic services and livelihood opportunities.

Since its establishment, the office has expanded its scope to include the monitoring of 154 urban poor communities across Lucena City. Over the years, OCUPA has handled the verification, documentation, and management of thousands of beneficiary records. Although the office adopted a Visual Basic–based record management system in earlier years, much of its verification and data processing remained manual, leading to challenges in accuracy and efficiency. This historical transition highlights the necessity for modernization and serves as the foundation for the development of the proposed digital information management system.

Officials

OCUPA is managed by a small team of officials responsible for handling administrative tasks, field verification, and beneficiary coordination. Led by the Officer-in-Charge, the staff consists of administrative aides and support personnel who work together to manage records, process applications, and assist urban poor communities across Lucena City.
